<p><p>Femoral arterial cannulae are specialized medical devices used in cardiac surgeries for establishing extracorporeal circulation. There are two main types of femoral arterial cannulae on the market: unipolar and bipolar. Unipolar femoral arterial cannulae have a single lumen for blood flow, while bipolar femoral arterial cannulae have two lumens for both blood inflow and outflow. Each type has specific advantages and is chosen based on the patient's individual needs and the requirements of the surgical procedure.</p></p>

<p><p>Femoral arterial cannulae are commonly used in traditional cardiopulmonary bypass surgery to provide arterial access for cardiopulmonary support. In Extracorporeal Membrane Pulmonary Oxygenation (ECMO), these cannulae are utilized to facilitate gas exchange and support failing lungs. In hemodialysis, they are used for vascular access during the filtration process. Additionally, femoral arterial cannulae find application in other markets such as critical care settings, trauma surgery, and emergency medicine where temporary arterial access is required for various interventions.</p></p>
