<p><p>Gas sensors for semiconductors can be classified into resistive and non-resistive types based on their sensing mechanisms. Resistive gas sensors change their electrical resistance in response to the presence of target gases, while non-resistive sensors generate signals through mechanisms such as electrochemical reactions, optical absorption, or capacitive changes. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in monitoring and controlling gas concentrations in semiconductor manufacturing processes to ensure product quality and worker safety.</p></p>
