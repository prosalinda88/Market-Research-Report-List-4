<p><p>Hand-held tonometers are devices used to measure the intraocular pressure of the eye. There are two main types in the market: contact type and non-contact type. Contact tonometers require direct contact with the eye to take a reading, while non-contact tonometers use a puff of air to measure eye pressure without touching the eye. Both types are used by healthcare professionals to diagnose and monitor conditions like glaucoma. The choice between contact and non-contact tonometers depends on the patient's comfort and specific needs.</p></p>
