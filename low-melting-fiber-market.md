<p><p>Low melting fibers can be categorized into two types based on their melting point. The first type has a melting point of 130 ℃ or lower, while the second type has a melting point higher than 130 ℃. Fibers with a melting point ≤130 ℃ are used in applications where low heat resistance is required, such as in disposable products. On the other hand, fibers with a melting point >130 ℃ are utilized in applications where higher heat resistance is necessary, such as in automotive and construction materials.</p></p>
