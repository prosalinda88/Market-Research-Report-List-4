<p><p>Hand-held veterinary refractometers are used to measure the concentration of a substance in a liquid sample. Optical refractometers are traditional devices that use light refraction to determine the concentration, whereas digital refractometers provide an accurate digital reading of the concentration. Optical refractometers are typically more affordable and commonly used in veterinary settings, while digital refractometers offer faster and more precise results. Both types play a crucial role in veterinary medicine for diagnosing conditions such as dehydration and kidney disease.</p></p>
