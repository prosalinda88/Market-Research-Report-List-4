<p><p>Flame retardants are chemical additives used in various materials to reduce flammability. Common types include ATH, which releases water vapor when exposed to high temperatures, Antimony Oxide, which inhibits the ignition process, Brominated, which disrupts the combustion process, Chlorinated, which interferes with the flame propagation, and Phosphorous, which forms a protective char layer. Each type of flame retardant has unique properties and uses in different applications to improve fire safety and reduce the risk of ignition and spread of flames.</p></p>
