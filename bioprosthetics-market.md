<p><p>Bioprosthetics refer to devices or materials that are derived from biological sources and used in medical procedures. The two main types of bioprosthetics in the market are allografts and xenografts. Allografts are tissues or organs that are taken from one individual and transplanted into another within the same species. Xenografts, on the other hand, involve the use of tissues or organs from a different species. Both types of bioprosthetics have their own advantages and limitations, depending on the specific medical application.</p></p>
