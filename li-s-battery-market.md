<p><p>A Li-S battery, short for Lithium-sulfur battery, is a type of rechargeable battery featuring a lithium metal anode and a sulfur cathode. One of the key driving factors for this growth is the increasing demand for high energy density batteries in electric vehicles and portable electronic devices. The Li-S battery offers a higher energy density compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries, making it an attractive option for these applications.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in sulfur cathode materials and electrolytes have addressed some of the challenges associated with the Li-S battery, such as low cycle life and poor rate performance. These technological improvements have further fueled the market growth of Li-S batteries.</p><p>In addition, research and development efforts are ongoing to enhance the performance and stability of Li-S batteries, with a focus on increasing energy density, improving cycle life, and reducing costs.
