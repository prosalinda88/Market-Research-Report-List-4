<p><p>Qualitative respirator fit testing involves three main types of tests: the saccharin or Bitrex test, the isoamyl acetate test, and the irritant smoke test. The saccharin or Bitrex test involves wearing a respirator while being exposed to a sweet or bitter aerosol to determine if the wearer can taste the substance. The isoamyl acetate test uses banana oil as the aerosol, while the irritant smoke test exposes the wearer to a stinging smoke to assess the respirator's effectiveness in protecting against irritants.</p></p>
