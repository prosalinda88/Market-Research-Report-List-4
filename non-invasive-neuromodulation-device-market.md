<p><strong>The Non-invasive Neuromodulation Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS)</li><li>Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS)</li><li>Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS)</li><li>Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS)</li><li>Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Non-invasive neuromodulation devices are used to stimulate the brain without requiring surgery. Types of these devices include Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS), which delivers constant low electrical currents to the brain; Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS), which delivers oscillating currents; Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS), which delivers intermittent pulses; Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS), which delivers random electrical noise; and Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), which uses electrical currents to induce seizures. Other types of non-invasive neuromodulation devices also exist in the market for various neurological applications.</p></p>
