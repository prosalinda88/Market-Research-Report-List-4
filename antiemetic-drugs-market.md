<p><p>Antiemetic drugs are used to prevent or alleviate nausea and vomiting. They can be classified into different types based on their mechanism of action. Dopamine antagonists work by blocking dopamine receptors in the brain. 5-HT3 receptor antagonists block serotonin receptors in the gut and in the chemoreceptor trigger zone. NK1 receptor antagonists target substance P receptors in the brain. Lastly, cannabinoid receptor antagonists act on the endocannabinoid system. Each of these types of antiemetic drugs target different pathways to effectively control nausea and vomiting.</p></p>
