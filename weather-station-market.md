<p><p>Weather stations are available in two main market types: wired and wireless. Wired weather stations require physical connections to sensors and a display unit, making installation more complex but generally more reliable. On the other hand, wireless weather stations use radio frequency to transmit data from sensors to a display unit, offering easier installation and flexibility in sensor placement. Both types have their advantages and drawbacks, but wireless weather stations are becoming more popular due to their convenience and versatility.</p></p>
